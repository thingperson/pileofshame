## The one drift, in detail

**`components/JustFiveMinutes.tsx:262` — "📋 Play Next" button uses a retired status label.**

- The button triages a game to `on-deck` (`handleTriage('on-deck')`). The locked, canon-facing label for `on-deck` is **"Up Next"** (every other surface — `constants.ts:46`, `TabNav.tsx:23`, and this file's *own* toast at L134 — uses "Up Next").
- "Play Next" is on the voice-charter retired list ("do not reintroduce: … Play Next, On Deck, …"). This is exactly the terminology-drift the status-cycle lock exists to prevent.
- **Severity: low.** One button label inside the "Just Five Minutes" triage flow, not a core-loop surface, and functionally correct (sets the right state). But it's an unauthorized deviation from a LOCKED decision with no superseding DECISIONS entry → classified ❌, not ⚠️.
- **Suggested fix (Brady's call — not applied):** change the button label at `JustFiveMinutes.tsx:262` from `📋 Play Next` to `🎯 Up Next` (matching the TabNav icon+label and the file's own toast).

Related note: the same file also labels the pile-return button "📚 Back to The Pile" and the exit "🚪 Not for me" — both fine ("The Pile" is a sanctioned casual synonym for backlog; "Not for me" is the canon move-on verb).
